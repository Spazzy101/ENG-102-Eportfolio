Learning Outcomes

A quick guide to the course outcomes directly connected to my showcase pieces.

Six Learning Outcomes Used Across This Portfolio

The reflections across my five showcase pieces directly connect my work to the following ENG 102 learning outcomes.

1. Define an argumentative purpose

I used this in my Discourse Community Analysis by arguing that communication, not just technical skiing ability, determines membership and expertise in Killington Ski School and Mountain Operations.

2. Adopt an open stance toward competing perspectives

I used this in my interview work by considering the different communication priorities of ski patrol and ski instruction.

3. Negotiate competing perspectives

I used this in my Stakeholder Mapping project by analyzing how instructors, patrol, mountain operations, guests, and trainees all have different interests and levels of power.

4. Exhibit flexibility for audiences and situations

I used this in the Rhetorical Analysis by explaining how a website requires a different structure, tone, and design than an academic essay.

5. Implement composing processes

I used invention, drafting, collaboration, revision, and reflection throughout the portfolio, especially in the interview and reflection process.

6. Evaluate and document sources

I used this in the Annotated Bibliography by analyzing sources from writing studies, ski patrol, and ski instruction to support my larger research argument.

Additional Outcomes Also Included

The portfolio also references identifying competing cultural logics, imagining equitable outcomes, demonstrating resilience while revising, and reflecting on fair use and copyright in public-facing academic work.
