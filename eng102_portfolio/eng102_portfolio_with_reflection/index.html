Portfolio Introduction

This portfolio highlights how my writing developed across ENG 102 through research, revision, reflection, and audience awareness.

Over the semester, I strengthened my ability to define an argumentative purpose that addresses an academic issue and to adopt an open stance toward competing perspectives. These skills became especially important as I worked within my chosen discourse community, Killington Ski School and Mountain Operations, where communication, safety, professionalism, and shared expectations shape how members participate.

One of the most important areas of growth for me was implementing composing processes such as invention, drafting, collaboration, revision, and reflection. Looking at my earlier assignments compared to later projects shows how much my writing improved through feedback and intentional revision. I also developed the ability to evaluate, incorporate, and document sources according to academic conventions, which helped strengthen my arguments and make my writing more credible.

In addition, I learned to exhibit flexibility when addressing different audiences and rhetorical situations. Some assignments required academic research, while others required me to think about public-facing communication and digital design. Each piece in this portfolio demonstrates a different aspect of that growth. Overall, this portfolio reflects not just what I wrote, but how I learned to think more critically, write more effectively, and apply these skills in future academic and professional contexts.

My Work This Semester

Throughout this semester, my writing developed from understanding a discourse community to analyzing how communication functions within it and how that knowledge can be applied in real-world contexts.

My work focuses on Killington Ski School and Mountain Operations, where I explored how members communicate, how newcomers learn expectations, and how writing plays a role in safety, instruction, and professionalism.

Each piece in this portfolio represents a different stage of that process, from early analysis to research to applying those ideas through audience and rhetorical decisions. Together, they show how my understanding of writing evolved over time and how I became more intentional in the way I approach communication.
