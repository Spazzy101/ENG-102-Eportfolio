Critical Reflection

The annotated bibliography helped me strengthen one of the most important academic writing skills in ENG 102: evaluating, incorporating, and documenting sources according to academic conventions. At first, I thought of the bibliography as simply a list of sources, but the assignment required more than that. I had to explain what each source argued, why it mattered, and how it connected to my research on Killington Ski School and Mountain Operations.

This piece shows my growth in discipline-specific research and argumentation because I used sources from writing studies, ski patrol training, and ski instruction. For example, Swales gave me the framework for understanding discourse communities, while National Ski Patrol materials helped me understand the importance of documentation and clear emergency communication. Devitt and Winsor helped me think about genre as something people learn by participating in a professional environment rather than only through formal instruction.

The assignment also pushed me to demonstrate resilience when drafting and revising academic arguments. My early thinking was mostly summary-based, but I had to revise toward analysis by explaining how each source supported my claim about communication and expertise. This will matter in future college and business writing because strong arguments depend on credible evidence. In finance and accounting, I will need to evaluate sources, explain relevance, and connect evidence to a larger purpose in a clear and professional way.
