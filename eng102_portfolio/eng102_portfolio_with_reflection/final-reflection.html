Final Reflection

My growth as a writer and what I take forward from ENG 102.

Over the course of ENG 102, my understanding of writing shifted from simply completing assignments to understanding writing as a tool for communication within specific contexts. Through my focus on Killington Ski School and Mountain Operations, I learned that writing is shaped by audience, purpose, and expectations rather than just structure or grammar.

One of my biggest areas of growth was developing an argumentative purpose and supporting it with evidence. Early on, my writing relied on general ideas, but through assignments like the annotated bibliography and discourse community analysis, I improved my ability to incorporate sources and explain their relevance. This connects directly to evaluating and documenting sources effectively.

I also developed stronger rhetorical awareness. Different assignments required me to adapt my writing for different audiences. The rhetorical analysis and website planning showed me that effective communication depends on clarity, organization, and understanding what the audience needs.

Another key improvement was my writing process. I learned that revision is essential. Comparing my earlier drafts to later work shows how my ideas became clearer and more developed. This reflects resilience in drafting and revising.

Overall, ENG 102 helped me become a more intentional writer. The skills I developed—critical thinking, research, audience awareness, and revision—will carry into my future academic work and my career in business.
