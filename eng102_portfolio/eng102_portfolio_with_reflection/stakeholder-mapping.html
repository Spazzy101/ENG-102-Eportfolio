Evidence from the map: Mountain Operations has high power because it controls terrain and lift access, while guests hold indirect but significant power through satisfaction, spending, and reputation.

Critical Reflection

The stakeholder mapping assignment helped me move beyond describing the discourse community and instead analyze how different groups interact within it. I identified ski instructors, ski patrol, mountain operations, guests, and prospective instructors or trainees as major stakeholders. This helped me understand that a discourse community is not flat; different groups have different levels of power, different interests, and different responsibilities.

This assignment connects strongly to the learning outcome of negotiating competing perspectives when addressing an academic issue. Ski instructors care about guest experience, teaching quality, certification, and work opportunities. Ski patrol focuses on safety, risk reduction, and emergency response. Mountain operations controls terrain availability and lift access, while guests influence reputation and demand. Prospective instructors depend on experienced members for training and entry into the community. Looking at all of these groups together helped me understand how one decision can affect several stakeholders at once.

The project also helped me imagine equitable outcomes that are open to academic and non-academic perspectives. For example, if newcomers struggle to understand communication expectations, the solution is not just to tell them to figure it out. A better approach would include clearer mentorship, examples, and feedback. This matters beyond ENG 102 because stakeholder analysis is also important in business. Understanding power, interests, and relationships will help me make better decisions in professional situations where different groups have different priorities.
