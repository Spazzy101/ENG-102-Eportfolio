Critical Reflection

This assignment became the foundation for my ENG 102 project because it forced me to define Killington Ski School and Mountain Operations as more than just a workplace or hobby. I had to explain it as a discourse community with shared goals, specialized language, and expectations for membership. In doing that, I developed the learning outcome of defining an argumentative purpose that addresses an academic issue. My purpose was to argue that communication is central to expertise in this community, not just skiing ability.

The strongest part of this piece is that it connects personal experience to academic concepts. I wrote about how instructors, patrol, mountain ops, and managers rely on clear communication to maintain safety and professionalism. I also connected this to Swales by explaining that newcomers do not immediately understand the language of the group; they learn it through observation, practice, and feedback. This helped me adopt an open stance toward competing perspectives because I had to consider both experienced members and newcomers.

This piece also shows rhetorical knowledge because I analyzed how communication changes depending on audience. Instructors simplify technical language for guests but use more precise PSIA terminology with supervisors and trainers. That helped me understand flexibility in rhetorical situations. In future writing, especially in business, I will need this same skill: recognizing the expectations of a group, learning its language, and adapting my communication to fit the audience and purpose.
